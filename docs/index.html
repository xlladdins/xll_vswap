Variance Swap Pricer

The payoff at expiration \( t_n \) is:

$$ \sigma^2 = \frac{1}{t_n - t_0} \sum_{j=0}^{n-1} \left( \frac{\Delta X_j}{X_j} \right)^2 $$

Using Taylor's expansion and choosing \( f''(x) = \frac{2}{x^2} \), we derive:

$$ \sum_{j=0}^{n-1} \left( \frac{\Delta X_j}{X_j} \right)^2 = 2\log\frac{X_n}{X_0} + \frac{2(X_n - X_0)}{z} + \sum_{j=0}^{n-1} \left( \frac{2}{X_j} - \frac{2}{z} \right)\Delta X_j + O\left( \left( \frac{\Delta X_j}{X_j} \right)^3 \right) $$

The first two terms resemble a European option payoff, while the sum can be replicated by dynamically trading futures on \( X \).
